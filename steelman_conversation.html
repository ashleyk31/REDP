Voter Debate

Steelman For

Argument 1
H.J.R.A No. A99 will reduce operating costs for retail feed sellers, allowing them to offer more competitive pricing. This benefits local farmers and pet owners, who depend on animal feed, thus strengthening the agricultural sector and local economy.
Counter 1
The measure could lead to decreased tax revenues, which local governments rely on for funding essential public services, potentially impacting community welfare.
Argument 2
Implementing this tax exemption now supports recovery from economic strains, providing timely relief that encourages expansion and investment in the agricultural supply chain.
Counter 2
This could set a precedent for further tax breaks, straining financial resources and encouraging more industries to seek exemptions, which could destabilize long-term fiscal health.
Argument 3
Evidence from regions with similar exemptions shows potential for increased market activity and sales, suggesting that this measure could stimulate local economic growth and job creation.
Counter 3
The economic dynamics in Texas differ, and positive impacts seen elsewhere might not replicate here, particularly if underlying issues like infrastructure and market access are not simultaneously addressed.

Steelman Against

Argument 1
The exemption will lead to decreased revenue for local governments, negatively affecting public services like education and infrastructure which rely on tax funding.
Counter 1
The reduction in tax revenue may be offset by economic growth, as lower costs for feed can reduce prices for consumers, stimulate spending, and ultimately boost local economies and tax generation in other areas.
Argument 2
The measure lacks specificity and oversight, potentially opening loopholes for tax avoidance and abuse by large retailers or unrelated commercial entities.
Counter 2
The legislature can develop stringent eligibility requirements and clear guidelines to target the exemption specifically toward small, independent feed retailers, minimizing potential misuse and ensuring oversight.
Argument 3
There is insufficient evidence that the tax exemption will lead to significant cost savings for consumers, as retailers may not pass these savings onto end customers.
